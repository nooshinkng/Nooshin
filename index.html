    • Exploring Multisensory Memory Evocation: A Comparative Analysis Through Marcel Proust's Novel and Roland Barthes within Henri Bergson's Theoretical Framework"

      MFA Thesis, Under the supervision of Dr. Hassan Khoobdel and Dr. Mohammad Hasan Pour
      2021, University of Soore, Tehran

      This research examines Marcel Proust's emphasis on multisensory reminiscence beyond visual aspects, encompassing all five senses . Proust's groundbreaking exploration extends beyond traditional visual memory, delving into the realms of touch, taste, smell, and sound. This holistic approach offers a comprehensive understanding of memory evocation . The comparative analysis reveals paradoxical viewpoints on this matter, with Barthes displaying heightened interest in photographs, while Proust perceives them as a recreation of voluntary memory, a concept he pioneered. Notably, this research uncovers subtle literary borrowings from Proust within Barthes' works, illustrating the enduring impact of Proust's multisensory exploration on subsequent thinkers in the realm of memory studies.


    • Analysis of Gender Representation and its Transformations in Qajar Paintings and Photographs

      Collaboration with Dr. Hassan Khoobdel Ph.D. Thesis.
      2021, Art University of Isfahan

      The visual art of the Naseri era blends traditional Qajar painting with influences from Western advancements like photography. This period's art reflects a complex interplay of forces, particularly in gender representation. While the portrayal of gender remains a focal point, it diverges significantly from earlier styles. This research examines the quality of gender representation in Naseri-era painting and photography, highlighting the distinct approaches of each medium. Using a descriptive-analytical method and historical comparison, the study explores how photography introduces novel methods, such as realism and unique perspectives, to portray gender.
